Objective Truth in Decision Modeling

The employment landscape is often obscured by complex labor codes, and corporations profit from wage minimization. Solveria exists to provide the Counter-Algorithm.

We believe that accurate, client-side modeling is a digital right. Our tools are engineered to provide "Base Reality" scenarios—unfiltered by marketing goals or sales quotas. When you calculate overtime violations or missed meal breaks on Solveria, you are seeing the math as it exists in the raw statutory data.

Zero-Trust Data Architecture

We do not rely on "industry estimates" or "rules of thumb." Our decision engines are indexed against primary government and statutory labor sources.

Employment Law Benchmarks

  • Federal Baselines: Our core all-in-one (AIO) tools are strictly indexed against the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), ensuring precise calculations for federal minimum wage and foundational overtime rules.
  • State-Specific Statutes: Our state-level applications reference primary state labor departments—such as California’s Division of Labor Standards Enforcement (DLSE) wage orders or New York’s Department of Labor (DOL) guidelines—to calculate exact local minimum wages, spread-of-hours pay, and premium penalties.

Editorial Independence

Solveria is an independent educational platform. Our mathematical outputs are firewalled from our revenue model.

  • No "Pay-to-Play" Math: A legal network cannot pay us to artificially inflate a user's potential wage claim just to generate a lead. The math is the math. If an input does not qualify for an overtime penalty under state law, our tool will flag it as zero, regardless of our affiliate partners.
  • Client-Side Execution: All calculations occur locally in your browser via JavaScript. This ensures that no external server can manipulate your specific results based on your profile or location.

Algorithmic Auditing & Updates

Labor laws and economic variables change frequently. Our code is audited on a strict schedule to ensure "Day 1" accuracy.

  • Quarterly Core Review: Every 90 days, our engineering team reviews the "Logic Core" of our core state-specific wage applications to ensure compatibility with new browser standards (ES6+) and security protocols.
  • Annual Minimum Wage Adjustments: On January 1st of each year, all base pay, cost-of-living adjustments, and state-specific minimum wage variables are hard-coded to the new statutory standards.
  • Immediate Statutory Patches: If a major legal precedent changes (e.g., California alters its PAGA penalties or meal break precedents), we deploy a hotfix to the relevant calculator within 48 hours of the ruling.

Accountability & Corrections

We value precision above all else. While our models are stress-tested, software is never perfect.

If you identify a discrepancy between a Solveria calculation and a verified Department of Labor audit or court judgment, please report it immediately to engineering@solveria.org. We investigate all reported logic errors within 24 hours and issue transparency logs for any algorithmic adjustments made.
