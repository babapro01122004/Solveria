Core Architecture & Standards

Solveria is engineered to be intuitive, but complex financial modeling requires context. Below you will find documentation on our calculation methodologies, data sources, and troubleshooting protocols.

Transparency & Methodology

Our Decision Engine utilizes the us-state-data protocol to reference local statutes, tax codes, and actuarial tables. While we strive for 100% accuracy, results should be viewed as high-fidelity estimates rather than binding appraisals.

Data Source Logic

  • Financial Tools: Updated monthly with Federal Reserve interest rate benchmarks and CPI inflation data to ensure forward-looking accuracy.
  • Legal Tools: Indexed against average settlement multipliers and tort reform caps specific to your state jurisdiction.
  • Tax Considerations: Algorithms account for standard deductions and progressive tax brackets based on the most recent IRS publications.

Technical Troubleshooting

Because Solveria operates on a Client-Side Architecture, performance relies on your browser's capabilities. This ensures your financial data is processed locally on your device and is never transmitted to external servers for calculation.

  • Calculators not loading? Ensure JavaScript is enabled. We recommend disabling aggressive ad-blockers that may interfere with our visualization scripts (Chart.js or D3.js libraries).
  • Data not saving? We use LocalStorage to persist your inputs for convenience. Clearing your browser cache or using Incognito Mode will reset your scenarios.

Report an Issue

Found a Data Discrepancy?
If you notice a calculation that conflicts with local regulations in your state, please file a report with our engineering team via the Contact page. We prioritize data integrity updates within 48 hours.
