How to Use Our Home Affordability Calculator

Follow these steps to estimate how much house you can afford:

  1. Enter Income & Debt: Provide your total annual household income and your total monthly debt payments (like car loans or student loans).
  2. Set DTI Limits: Enter the maximum Front-End (housing) and Back-End (total debt) DTI ratios you're comfortable with. Lenders often use 28% and 36% as guidelines.
  3. Add Purchase Details: Input your saved down payment, desired loan term, estimated interest rate, and estimated closing costs as a percentage.
  4. Estimate Ongoing Costs: Provide estimates for annual property tax, homeowners insurance, monthly HOA fees, and annual maintenance.
  5. Analyze Your Results: The calculator will instantly show your affordable home price, loan amount, one-time closing costs, and a detailed breakdown of your total monthly payment.

Understanding Your Affordability Results

Your results are broken down into four key areas to give you a complete financial picture:

  • One-Time Costs: This is the total cash you'll need at closing. It includes your down payment plus the estimated closing costs (typically 2-5% of the home price) for fees like appraisal, title, and origination.
  • Loan & Ratios: This shows the total loan amount you can borrow. It also confirms the Front-End (housing-only) and Back-End (total debt) DTI ratios that your affordability estimate is based on.
  • Annual Costs: A yearly summary of your non-mortgage expenses. This helps you budget for large, predictable costs like property taxes and insurance.
  • Monthly House Costs: This is your estimated total monthly "cost of living" in the home. It includes your mortgage payment (P&I) plus the monthly portion of taxes, insurance, HOA fees, and maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: What is Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio?
A1: DTI is your total monthly debt payments divided by your gross monthly income. Lenders use it to assess your ability to manage monthly payments. There are two types:
- Front-End DTI: Your estimated total housing payment (PITI + HOA) as a percentage of your gross monthly income.
- Back-End DTI: Your total housing payment plus all other monthly debts (car loans, credit cards, etc.) as a percentage of your gross monthly income.
Q2: What is PITI?
A2: PITI stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. These are the four main components of a monthly mortgage payment. Our calculator also includes HOA fees and maintenance to give you a more complete picture of total homeownership costs.
Q3: How much should I budget for closing costs and maintenance?
A3: Closing Costs typically range from 2% to 5% of the home's purchase price. Maintenance costs can vary, but a common rule of thumb is to budget 1% to 1.5% of the home's value per year for ongoing repairs and upkeep.
Q4: Does this calculator guarantee I'll be approved for this loan amount?
A4: No. This calculator provides a powerful and reliable estimate based on the numbers you provide. Your final loan approval, interest rate, and affordable amount are subject to a formal underwriting process from a lender, which includes verifying your credit score, income, assets, and the property's value.
